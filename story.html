The Constraint

One laptop, $300 in cloud credits—Sylhet was already under water. GDACS flood maps came 72 hours late. Government relief teams navigated by rumour. We had Sentinel-1 SAR tiles, an Nvidia 1660 Ti, and 96 hours to ship something that mattered.

91% mIoU

The Physics

Backscatter alone lies. We fused SAR coherence, NDWI from Sentinel-2, and SRTM slope into a three-layer stack—then trained a U-Net++ that respected hydrology. The model learned water doesn't climb hills. That constraint turned 78% accuracy into 91% mIoU. Physics beat pixels.

336× faster

The Arbitrage of Attention

Google and Meta pre-train foundation models on ImageNet—99% of which has zero flood pixels. We pre-trained on NASA HLS + ESA SAR archives: 2M images of Brahmaputra basin morphology. When disaster struck, we fine-tuned in 6 hours, not 6 weeks. Big Tech optimises for Fortune-500 margins; we mine the 6 dB signal they overlook.

2M images

The Distribution Problem

Relief vans don't have fibre. Clinics can't send patient data to AWS. Factories won't expose their shop-floor to the cloud. So we quantised models to INT8, deployed them on Raspberry Pi 4Bs, and built federated training loops that aggregate gradients without centralising sensitive data. The system improves itself—offline.

<120ms P95

The Outcome

Four production systems now run across geospatial, industrial, health, and public sectors. They process 2TB/day on infrastructure that costs less than a single GPU node in Oregon. Insight dies in slide decks. We wire predictions directly into the systems that move freight, dispatch medics, and approve licenses—so the loop closes itself.
